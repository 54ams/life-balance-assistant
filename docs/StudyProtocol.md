# Study Protocol

## Objective

Evaluate feasibility, usability, and interpretability of the Life Balance Assistant prototype.

## Recruitment

- Convenience sample, target 5-8 adults
- Inclusion: owns smartphone and can perform daily check-in for 7-14 days
- Exclusion: any situation requiring clinical monitoring

## Procedure

1. Provide Privacy Notice and obtain app consent.
2. Optional WHOOP connection and consent.
3. Daily use for 7-14 days:
   - Daily check-in
   - Optional wearable sync
   - Review plan and mark completed actions
4. End-of-study:
   - SUS questionnaire in app
   - Export anonymised research JSON

## Outcomes

- Primary: SUS score, completion/adherence rate
- Secondary: perceived understanding of LBI and action usefulness
- Exploratory: adherence vs LBI associations

## Safety and ethics

- Not medical advice; no diagnosis.
- Safety signposting available in consent and dedicated safety resources screen.
- Consent withdrawal disables insights and WHOOP sync.
- User can delete all data and run retention purge.
